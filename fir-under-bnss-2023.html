FIR Under BNSS 2023

By Nikhil Chugh, Advocate | Supreme Court Bar Association Member | Panipat District Court

The Bharatiya Nagarik Suraksha Sanhita, 2023 (BNSS) has replaced the Criminal Procedure Code. Under Section 173 BNSS, registration of a First Information Report (FIR) in cognizable offences is mandatory. The new law strengthens electronic filing of FIRs and enhances procedural safeguards for citizens.

1. Section 173 BNSS – Registration of FIR

Every information relating to a cognizable offence must be recorded by the police. If the information is given orally, it must be reduced into writing and signed by the informant. A copy of the FIR must be provided free of cost.

2. Electronic FIR (e-FIR)

BNSS formally recognises electronic communication. FIR may be filed digitally and must be signed within three days. This ensures faster and more accessible justice delivery.

3. Zero FIR

Zero FIR allows registration at any police station irrespective of territorial jurisdiction. It ensures immediate police action without delay.

4. Refusal to Register FIR – Legal Remedy

Under Section 174 BNSS, if police refuse to register FIR, a complaint may be made to the Superintendent of Police. The Magistrate also has power to order investigation where necessary.

5. Preliminary Inquiry Under BNSS

For offences punishable between three to seven years, preliminary inquiry may be conducted before registration in certain cases.

6. Quashing of FIR

High Courts retain inherent powers to quash FIR where continuation of proceedings would amount to abuse of process of law.
