Top 5 Legal Rights Everyone Must Know in India

By Nikhil Chugh, Advocate | Supreme Court Bar Association Member | Panipat District Court

← Back to Legal Insights

Legal awareness is the foundation of personal liberty. Every citizen in India must understand their constitutional and procedural rights during arrest, police investigation, and criminal proceedings. Awareness of these rights prevents misuse of authority and safeguards dignity under law.

1. Right to Know the Grounds of Arrest

Under Article 22 of the Constitution of India and Section 47 of the Bharatiya Nagarik Suraksha Sanhita, 2023, a person must be informed of the reasons for arrest. Police cannot detain an individual without clearly communicating the charges.

2. Right Against Self-Incrimination

Article 20(3) guarantees that no person accused of an offence shall be compelled to be a witness against themselves. Confessions obtained through coercion are not admissible in court.

3. Right to Legal Representation

Every accused person has the right to consult and be defended by a lawyer of their choice. If a person cannot afford legal representation, free legal aid must be provided under constitutional principles.

4. Right to Bail in Bailable Offences

In bailable offences, bail is a statutory right. The accused may seek release from the police station itself, subject to furnishing required sureties.

5. Right to Register an FIR

Under Section 173 of the Bharatiya Nagarik Suraksha Sanhita, 2023, registration of FIR in cognizable offences is mandatory. Police authorities cannot refuse to record such complaints.

Understanding these five essential legal rights strengthens democratic values and ensures that justice is not compromised. Citizens who are aware of their rights are better equipped to protect themselves and seek appropriate legal remedies.
