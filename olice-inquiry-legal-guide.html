What To Do If Police Call You for Inquiry? – Complete Legal Guide (2026)

By Nikhil Chugh, Advocate | Supreme Court Bar Association Member | Panipat District Court

Receiving a call from the police can be stressful. Many people panic immediately, assuming they are about to be arrested. However, not every police inquiry means you are accused of a crime.

Understanding your legal rights and responding calmly can protect you from unnecessary trouble. This guide explains exactly what you should do if police call you for inquiry in India.

1. Do Not Panic

A police inquiry call does not automatically mean you are guilty or about to be arrested. Police often call individuals for clarification, witness statements, or preliminary investigation.

Stay calm and gather proper information before taking any step.

2. Ask Basic Details Politely

Before visiting the police station, you should ask:

  • Your name and designation
  • Police station name
  • Purpose of inquiry
  • Whether you are being called as a witness or suspect

You have the right to know why you are being called.

3. You Cannot Be Forced Without Legal Procedure

Under Indian law, police must follow proper procedure before compelling someone to appear. In many cases, they are required to issue a written notice under Section 41A of the Criminal Procedure Code (CrPC) for appearance.

If no formal notice is issued, you may consult a lawyer before visiting.

4. Do Not Give Written Statements Without Legal Advice

Never sign any statement without reading and understanding it completely. If you feel pressured, politely state that you wish to consult your lawyer.

This protects you from misinterpretation or future complications.

5. You Have the Right to Legal Representation

You are entitled to consult an advocate before making any statement. Even during questioning, legal guidance ensures your rights are not violated.

Seeking early legal advice can often prevent escalation into arrest or formal charges.

6. Understand the Difference Between Inquiry and Arrest

An inquiry is part of investigation. Arrest requires legal grounds and procedure. Police cannot arrest arbitrarily without justification.

If you fear arrest, consult a lawyer immediately regarding anticipatory bail options.

7. Avoid Arguments or Aggressive Behaviour

Maintain respectful communication. Non-cooperation or aggression can worsen the situation.

Cooperate lawfully, but remain aware of your rights.

When Should You Immediately Contact a Lawyer?

  • If police insist on immediate appearance without notice
  • If you suspect false allegations
  • If you are being pressured to sign documents
  • If there is risk of arrest

Early legal intervention often makes a significant difference.

Final Advice

A police inquiry is not a conviction. It is part of the investigative process. The key is to respond legally, calmly, and strategically.

If you receive such a call and are unsure about your next step, seek professional legal advice before proceeding.

Being informed is your first layer of protection.
