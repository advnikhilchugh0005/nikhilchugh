What To Do If Police Call You for Inquiry?

By Nikhil Chugh, Advocate | Supreme Court Bar Association Member | Panipat District Court

Receiving a call from the police can be stressful. Many people immediately assume they are about to be arrested. However, not every police inquiry means you are accused of a crime.

Understanding your legal rights and responding calmly can protect you from unnecessary trouble. This guide explains exactly what you should do if police call you for inquiry in India.

1. Do Not Panic

A police inquiry call does not automatically mean you are guilty. Police often call individuals for clarification, witness statements, or preliminary investigation.

2. Ask Basic Details Politely

Before visiting the police station, ask:

  • Name and designation of the officer
  • Name of the police station
  • Purpose of inquiry
  • Whether you are being called as witness or suspect

3. Are You Legally Bound to Go?

Under Indian law, especially Section 35 of BNSS 2023, police cannot arbitrarily detain or arrest someone without proper grounds. If you receive a written notice under Section 41A (now BNSS equivalent), you must comply.

4. Right to Legal Representation

You have the right to consult a lawyer before making any statement. It is always advisable to inform your advocate before appearing for inquiry.

5. Do Not Sign Anything Without Reading

Never sign blank papers or statements without understanding the content. If required, seek legal advice before signing.

6. Women & Special Protections

Women cannot be called to police station after sunset except in exceptional circumstances. Police must follow specific procedural safeguards.

7. When Should You Be Concerned?

If police threaten arrest without explanation or pressure you to confess, immediately contact a lawyer. Legal remedies such as anticipatory bail may be available.
