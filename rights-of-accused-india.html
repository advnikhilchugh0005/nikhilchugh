Rights of an Accused in India

By Nikhil Chugh, Advocate | Supreme Court Bar Association Member | Panipat District Court

In India, every accused person is presumed innocent until proven guilty. The Constitution of India and criminal procedural laws ensure that no individual is deprived of liberty without due process of law. Understanding the rights of an accused is essential to protect personal liberty and prevent misuse of power.

1. Right to Equality Before Law (Article 14)

Every accused person is entitled to equal protection of the law without discrimination based on religion, caste, gender or social status.

2. Right to Be Informed of Charges (Article 22 & Section 50 CrPC)

The accused must be informed about the grounds of arrest and the specific charges against them.

3. Right to Legal Representation

An accused has the right to consult and be defended by a lawyer of their choice. Free legal aid is provided if the accused cannot afford counsel.

4. Right Against Self-Incrimination (Article 20(3))

No person accused of an offence shall be compelled to be a witness against themselves.

5. Right to Bail

In bailable offences, bail is a matter of right. In non-bailable offences, courts evaluate the seriousness of the offence and other factors.

6. Right to Fair and Speedy Trial (Article 21)

The right to life and personal liberty includes the right to a fair, impartial and speedy trial.

7. Protection Against Double Jeopardy (Article 20(2))

No person shall be prosecuted and punished for the same offence more than once.

8. Protection Against Arbitrary Arrest

Police must produce the accused before a Magistrate within 24 hours and follow proper legal procedures.
